RED GARDEN

A deck-based real-time strategy game: draw cards, deploy autonomous units onto a living cellular-automata board, and watch systems collide. Clash Royale's card economy, Conway's Game of Life underneath it.

What this is

You don't control units directly — you introduce pressure onto a board that's alive on its own tick, independent of anything you do. Every cell has a state (Neutral, Player, Enemy, Corrupted) that spreads and reacts on a 2-second automata clock, whether or not you're looking at it. Card-hand deployment, LoL-style affordances, real-time not turn-based, PC first. Desktop is the whole focus right now — no mobile build yet.

What's actually built (not a pitch, the real state)

  • VS0Bot-vs-bot matches — the living grid, four starting cards, a working tech tree, a real win condition. Validated headless, end to end.
  • VS1Online play — matchmaking, connect-ticket accounts, validated with ten independent bots holding five concurrent matches under sustained load, zero crashes.
  • VS2The MOBA, live — a real click-to-move 10v10 arena with an 11-hero roster (drawn from EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL's own multiverse lore — real Irish and Norse myth, real historical figures) and a territory-control system contested by team presence. Not a pitch: a persistent bot pool is running real matches on this build right now.
  • NextRanked play — a rating system is designed (plain ELO), not yet built. More heroes queued.
